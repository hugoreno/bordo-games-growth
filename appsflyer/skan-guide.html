The Problem

Since iOS 14.5, Apple requires users to explicitly opt in to ad tracking via App Tracking Transparency (ATT). About 70-85% of users decline. This means traditional attribution (using Apple's device ID, IDFA) only works for a small minority.

SKAdNetwork is Apple's answer: a privacy-preserving framework built into iOS that lets you measure ad campaign performance without any user identifier. It works for 100% of users, regardless of their ATT consent.

Key insight: SKAN is not optional. It's the primary way to measure iOS ad performance for the vast majority of your users. AppsFlyer is the tool that makes SKAN data actionable.

How SKAN Works — The Full Flow

Click each step to see details.

1

Ad is displayed in a publisher app

Your ad shows in another app (e.g., a game). iOS registers the ad view.

The publisher app shows your ad and starts a 3-second timer. If the ad is displayed for at least 3 seconds, SKAdNetwork registers this as a valid ad impression. The ad is cryptographically signed by the ad network, containing campaign info but no user identifiers.
2

User installs & launches your app

Must happen within 30 days. iOS attributes the install to the ad.

If the user downloads and opens your app within the 30-day attribution window, iOS matches the install to the ad impression. This happens entirely on-device — no data leaves the phone at this stage. The attribution logic runs inside iOS itself, not on any server.
3

Your app reports conversion values

AppsFlyer SDK encodes user activity into a 6-bit number (0–63).

As the user interacts with your app (makes purchases, completes levels, subscribes), the AppsFlyer SDK updates the conversion value based on your configured schema. This is a 6-bit number (0–63) that encodes whatever metrics you chose to measure. The CV can only go up, never down — iOS enforces this. The activity window starts at first launch.
4

Measurement window closes

After the time window expires, the conversion value is locked.

In SKAN 4, there are three windows: 0-2 days, 3-7 days, and 8-35 days. Once a window closes, its conversion value is locked and can't change. The first window gives you fine-grained data (0–63). Windows 2 and 3 only provide coarse values (low, medium, high). This is Apple's trade-off between measurement and privacy.
5

iOS sends postback (with random delay)

A data packet goes to the ad network + AppsFlyer. No user info included.

iOS adds a random delay of 24-48 hours after the window closes before sending the postback. This delay is intentional — it prevents anyone from correlating the postback timing with a specific user's install. The postback contains: campaign ID, conversion value, source app — but zero user-identifying information. AppsFlyer receives a copy of every postback.
6

AppsFlyer decodes & displays the data

Raw CVs are decoded into meaningful metrics on your dashboard.

AppsFlyer receives the postback, decodes the conversion value using your schema (e.g., CV 42 = "spent $5-$10, completed tutorial, logged in"), and presents it in your dashboard. They also apply machine learning models to estimate values for installs that fell below privacy thresholds and received null CVs. This is called "modeled data."

Privacy Thresholds — The Catch

Apple won't send full conversion values unless your campaign generates enough installs. This prevents identifying individual users from small campaigns. Drag the slider to see what data you get at different volumes.

The Chicken-and-Egg Problem

You need campaign data to optimize and scale. But you need scale to get campaign data. This is the fundamental challenge of SKAN.

Practical strategies:

  • Consolidate campaigns — fewer, broader campaigns reach thresholds faster
  • Use coarse values — SKAN 4's low/medium/high values kick in at lower thresholds than fine values
  • Leverage modeled data — AppsFlyer uses ML to estimate values for installs with null CVs
  • Combine with ATT data — use consented users (~15-30%) to calibrate your models

Key Decisions for Your CTO Call

1. What to encode in your 64 conversion values?

▼

This is the single most important decision. Your 64 values must encode everything you want to know about a user's first interactions with your app.

Revenue-focused schema
  • 64 revenue buckets for precise ROAS
  • Best for subscription/IAP apps
  • Directly measures monetization
  • Simpler to set up and interpret
Engagement-focused schema
  • Track funnels, levels, features
  • Better for ad-monetized apps
  • Richer behavioral insights
  • Harder to tie to revenue
Recommendation: For most apps, a split approach (3 bits revenue + 3 bits key events) gives the best balance. Use AppsFlyer's schema templates as a starting point.

2. Measurement window duration strategy

▼

How long do you measure in Window 1? Longer = more data but slower campaign optimization.

Shorter window (12–24 hrs)
  • Get data faster (postback ~3 days)
  • Optimize campaigns sooner
  • Good for apps with fast engagement
Longer window (48 hrs)
  • Captures more user actions
  • Better for slow-burn apps
  • Data arrives ~4-5 days after install

3. Campaign consolidation vs. granularity

▼

More campaigns = more targeting but risk falling below privacy thresholds. Fewer campaigns = more data per campaign.

Fewer, broader campaigns
  • More installs per campaign
  • Better chance of exceeding thresholds
  • Richer conversion value data
Many targeted campaigns
  • Granular audience targeting
  • Risk of null CVs everywhere
  • May need 128+ installs/day each
Watch out: Facebook recommends 128+ installs/day per campaign to get meaningful data. If you're spending small, consolidate.

4. AdAttributionKit — Apple's successor

▼

Apple announced AdAttributionKit as the next-gen replacement. It ships with iOS 18.4 and adds significant improvements.

  • Configurable attribution windows — customize per campaign
  • Re-engagement attribution — measure returning users (SKAN can't)
  • Country codes in postbacks — geo data (subject to thresholds)
  • Configurable cooldown periods — more control over timing
Action item: Ask your CTO about the migration timeline. SKAN and AdAttributionKit will coexist for a while, but new features only come to the new framework.

5. Dual strategy: SKAN + ATT-consented data

▼

15-30% of users do consent to tracking. This "IDFA" data is full-fidelity, user-level attribution. SKAN covers the rest.

  • Use consented data to calibrate and validate your SKAN conversion value schemas
  • Use consented data for detailed cohort analysis and LTV modeling
  • Use SKAN for broad campaign measurement across all users
  • AppsFlyer's modeled data uses consented users to fill SKAN gaps

Quick Knowledge Check

Test your understanding before the call. Click an answer for each question.

1. Why does SKAN exist?

To replace Google Analytics for iOS apps
To measure ad performance without user-identifying data, since most users decline tracking
To help Apple sell more ad space in the App Store
SKAN exists because ~75% of users decline IDFA tracking after iOS 14.5. Apple built SKAN so advertisers can still measure campaign effectiveness in a privacy-preserving way — no device IDs, no user-level data.

2. What is a conversion value?

The revenue amount from a single user
A unique user ID assigned by Apple
A 6-bit number (0–63) that you configure to encode post-install activity
A conversion value is a 6-bit number giving you 64 possible values. You define what each value means (revenue buckets, events, engagement levels). It's the only post-install data you get from SKAN — making schema design critical.

3. What happens if your campaign has too few installs?

Apple charges a penalty fee
You get null or only coarse (low/medium/high) conversion values instead of fine-grained data
SKAN stops tracking entirely for that campaign
Apple's privacy thresholds require enough installs to prevent identifying individuals. Below the threshold: you get null CVs (SKAN 3) or only coarse values like low/medium/high (SKAN 4). This is why campaign consolidation matters.

4. How quickly do you get SKAN data after a user installs?

In real time
Within 1 hour
At least 3–4 days for Window 1 data (measurement period + random 24-48hr delay)
Window 1 measures for 0-2 days, then iOS adds a random 24-48 hour delay before sending the postback. So the earliest you see data is ~3 days after install. Windows 2 and 3 are even later. This is a major limitation for real-time campaign optimization.

5. What is AppsFlyer's main role in SKAN?

It replaces SKAN with its own attribution system
It helps configure conversion values, collects postbacks, decodes data, and uses ML to fill data gaps
It handles all communication with Apple on your behalf
AppsFlyer sits on top of Apple's SKAN framework. It provides: Conversion Studio (design your schema), postback collection, CV decoding (raw numbers → business metrics), SKAN dashboard, and modeled data (ML estimates for null values). It makes raw SKAN data actionable.

Honest Limitations to Remember

No real-time data

Minimum 24-48hr delay on all postbacks. You cannot optimize campaigns in real time.

No user-level data

Everything is aggregated at campaign level. No individual user journeys.

64-value cap

Forces hard trade-offs on what you measure. You can't track everything.

Privacy thresholds

Low-volume campaigns get limited or no conversion data.

No re-engagement

SKAN only measures first install. No returning/churned user attribution.

Fraud vulnerability

Concentrated attribution creates a target for ad fraud manipulation.
